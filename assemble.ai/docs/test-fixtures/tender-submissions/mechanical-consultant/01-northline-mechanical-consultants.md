# Tender Submission

Project: Lighthouse Residences
Package: Mechanical Services Consultant
Tenderer: Northline Mechanical Consultants Pty Ltd
Date: 11 May 2026
Currency: AUD, ex GST

## 1. Tender Summary

Northline Mechanical Consultants submits this fee proposal for mechanical engineering design services for the proposed Class 2 residential development at Lighthouse Residences.

Our offer is based on the Request for Tender, architectural information issued for tender, the preliminary basement layouts, and the nominated design programme.

Total lump sum fee, excluding GST: $156,000

## 2. Base Scope Included

The base fee includes mechanical engineering design services for:

- Apartment air conditioning systems using split ducted or multi-head split system solutions, subject to final architectural coordination.
- Base building lobby, corridor, common area and amenities ventilation.
- Basement car park ventilation design, including jet fan layout concept, exhaust riser coordination and make-up air paths.
- CO monitoring and control strategy for basement car park ventilation.
- Natural ventilation review to apartments, corridors and common areas where nominated by the architectural design.
- Mechanical services design coordination with fire, electrical, hydraulic, structural and architectural disciplines.
- Design participation at fortnightly consultant coordination meetings.
- Mechanical services design documentation suitable for tender and construction certificate submission.
- Review of contractor shop drawings and technical submissions during construction.

## 7. Qualifications and Clarifications

1. The fee assumes one basement level and one rooftop mechanical plant zone.
2. The fee assumes no commercial kitchen exhaust system.
3. NatHERS modelling is excluded.
4. Smoke hazard management engineering is excluded.
5. Acoustic engineering advice is excluded, although we will coordinate with the acoustic consultant.
6. The fee assumes the architectural layout remains broadly consistent with the tender documents.
7. Additional redesign after DA or planning authority changes will be treated as a variation.
8. The number of meetings and inspections is limited to the allowances stated above.
