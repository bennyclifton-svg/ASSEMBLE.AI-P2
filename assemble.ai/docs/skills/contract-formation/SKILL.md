# Contract Formation Skill

## Tier 3 — Contract Reference Material Loaded

This skill contains clause reference tables for the four major Australian standard form contracts used in private construction. It enables the Procurement Agent to draft preliminary special conditions that amend or supplement the standard form.

**Always include this warning on any special conditions output:**
> "These preliminary special conditions are drafted for legal review. They should be reviewed by a construction lawyer before inclusion in the tender documents or execution of the contract."

---

## When to Load

Load this skill when:
- Drafting preliminary special conditions for an RFT
- Comparing contract forms to recommend the most appropriate form
- Explaining a clause to the user before contract execution
- Preparing the contract execution checklist

## Contract Form Selection Guide

| Project Type | Recommended Form | Notes |
|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| Construct-only, standard risk allocation | **AS 4000-1997** | Most current, balanced, widely used on private projects |
| Construct-only, older projects / contractor preference | **AS 2124-1992** | Still common; slightly more contractor-friendly than AS 4000 |
| Design and Construct | **AS 4902-2000** | D&C version of AS 4000; contractor takes design liability |
| Simple/smaller works | **ABIC MW-1** | Architect-administered; suitable for works <$5M |
| Government projects | State-specific forms | NSW: GC21 (preferred for government) — separate reference required |

## AS 4000-1997 — Key Clause Reference Table

The most commonly used form on private commercial and residential projects.

| Topic | Clause | Key Provisions |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Superintendent's role** | 20 | Superintendent acts as Principal's agent except when required to act independently (certifying, assessing). Must act honestly and fairly when certifying. |
| **Security** | 5 | Bank guarantees or retention. Amount: typically 5% of contract sum. |
| **Insurance — Contractor** | 17 | Works insurance (agreed sum), public liability (≥$10M — typically $20M+), workers compensation. |
| **Insurance — Principal** | 18 | Principal must maintain professional indemnity if Principal-furnished design. |
| **Programme** | 33 | Contractor submits baseline programme within [X] days of award. Updates [monthly / on request]. |
| **Subcontracting** | 9 | Contractor may subcontract but remains responsible. Nominated subcontractors provisions. |
| **Variations — Direction** | 36.1 | Superintendent may direct variations. Must be in writing. |
| **Variations — Valuation** | 36.3 | By agreement; failing agreement: contract rates where applicable, otherwise reasonable rates; or by daywork. |
| **Variations — Disputed** | 36.5 | Contractor proceeds with variation even if disputed. Dispute resolution later. |
| **Extension of Time** | 34 | Contractor must give notice within 28 days of the cause becoming apparent. Qualifying causes: Principal's acts/omissions, latent conditions, industrial action, weather. |
| **EOT — Assessment** | 34.4 | Superintendent assesses EOT; must grant if qualifying cause established. No negative EOT (cannot reduce contract time). |
| **Latent Conditions** | 25 | Contractor gives notice within 28 days. Entitlement if conditions differ materially from those a contractor could reasonably have anticipated. |
| **Payment Claims** | 37.1 | Contractor submits payment claim monthly (or per agreed schedule). |
| **Payment Certification** | 37.2 | Superintendent certifies within 14 days of receiving claim. |
| **Payment — Principal** | 37.3 | Principal pays within 28 days of receiving claim (or per Security of Payment Act — whichever sooner). |
| **Retention** | 37.4 | Typically 5% to 50% of contract sum, then 2.5% to PC. Released at PC (half) and end of DLP (remainder). |
| **Practical Completion** | 34.6 | Superintendent issues PC certificate when works substantially complete. Defects list permitted at PC. |
| **Defects Liability Period** | 35 | Typically 12 months from PC. Contractor obliged to rectify defects notified during DLP. |
| **Dispute Resolution** | 42 | Expert determination → arbitration. |
| **Termination by Principal** | 39 | For default (7 days notice to remedy); for convenience (14 days notice, contractor entitled to costs + loss of profit). |
| **Liquidated Damages** | 34.7 | Per day/week rate agreed in contract. Deducted from payment certificates after PC date. Cap often included. |
| **Suspension** | 34.5 | Principal may suspend for convenience. Contractor entitled to costs of suspension. |

## AS 2124-1992 — Key Clause Reference Table

Older form, still widely used. More detailed on some matters than AS 4000.

| Topic | Clause | Key Provisions |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Superintendent's role** | 23 | Acts as Principal's agent except where required to act independently. |
| **Security** | 6 | Bank guarantee or retention. |
| **Insurance** | 18–20 | Works, public liability, workers comp requirements. |
| **Programme** | 33 | Required within [X] days of award. |
| **Variations — Direction** | 40.1 | Superintendent directs in writing. |
| **Variations — Valuation** | 40.5 | Agreement; contract rates; reasonable rates; daywork. |
| **Extension of Time** | 35 | Notice within a reasonable time. Qualifying causes include: Principal default, latent conditions, industrial action, weather beyond seasonal average. |
| **Latent Conditions** | 12 | Conditions materially different from those that ought reasonably to have been anticipated. |
| **Payment Claims** | 42.1 | Monthly or as agreed. |
| **Payment Certification** | 42.1 | Superintendent certifies within 14 days. |
| **Payment** | 42.1 | Principal pays within 28 days of claim (note: AS 2124 pre-dates Security of Payment Act — Security of Payment Act timeframes govern). |
| **Retention** | 42.2 | Typically 10% to 5% of contract sum, then 5% to PC. |
| **Practical Completion** | 42.5 | PC when works complete except minor defects. Superintendent issues certificate. |
| **Defects Liability** | 37 | DLP from PC. Contractor rectifies defects notified during DLP. |
| **Liquidated Damages** | 36 | Per diem rate in Annexure. |
| **Dispute Resolution** | 47 | Expert determination or arbitration. |
| **Termination** | 44 | For default or convenience. |
| **Nominated Subcontractors** | 9A | More detailed than AS 4000 on nominated sub provisions. |

## AS 4902-2000 — Key Clause Reference Table

Design and Construct version. Contractor takes design responsibility.

| Topic | Clause | Key Provisions |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Design obligations** | 8 | Contractor responsible for all design under the contract. Design must be fit for purpose stated in client's Project Requirements. |
| **Fitness for purpose** | 8.1 | Implied warranty that design will be fit for purpose — higher obligation than reasonable care. |
| **Superintendent's role** | 20 | Same as AS 4000 but Superintendent cannot direct variations to design (only scope changes). |
| **Consultant novation** | 6 | Principal's consultants may be novated to contractor at award. Contractor then administers them. |
| **Variations** | 36 | Scope changes only — Superintendent cannot direct design variations. Design changes within contractor's risk. |
| **EOT** | 34 | Same qualifying causes as AS 4000. Design delays caused by contractor's own consultants are contractor's risk. |
| **Payment** | 37 | Same as AS 4000. |
| **Practical Completion** | 34.6 | Must comply with all design obligations, not just physical completion. |
| **Defects** | 35 | Extended DLP obligations — design latent defects may extend beyond standard DLP. |
| **IP / Ownership of design** | 8.4 | Contractor retains IP in design; Principal gets licence to use. Ensure licence is broad enough for future works. |

## ABIC MW-1 — Key Clause Reference Table

Simple Works Contract. Architect-administered. Suitable for smaller works.

| Topic | Clause | Key Provisions |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Architect's role** | A5 | Architect acts as administrator; not the same as AS 4000 Superintendent — more limited powers. |
| **Variations** | D4 | Architect issues variation orders. Contractor must proceed. |
| **Extensions of time** | C3 | Notice required promptly. Qualifying causes: Principal's acts, adverse weather, industrial action. |
| **Progress claims** | E2 | Contractor submits monthly. Architect certifies within 10 business days. |
| **Practical completion** | C4 | Architect issues PC certificate. |
| **Defects** | C5 | DLP typically 12 months. |
| **Dispute resolution** | K | Expert determination. |
| **Termination** | J | For default or convenience. |

## Preliminary Special Conditions — Drafting Templates

These are the most commonly required special conditions. Adapt as needed and always flag for legal review.

### 1. Liquidated Damages

```
SPECIAL CONDITION [X] — LIQUIDATED DAMAGES

Clause [34.7 / 36 / C3] of the General Conditions is amended as follows:

The rate of liquidated damages shall be $[amount] per [day / week] 
for each day/week the Contractor fails to achieve Practical Completion 
by the Date for Practical Completion.

The maximum aggregate amount of liquidated damages payable shall be 
[X]% of the Contract Sum.

Note: LD rate should be a genuine pre-estimate of the Principal's 
loss — not a penalty. Legal review required to confirm rate is defensible.
```

### 2. Defects Liability Period

```
SPECIAL CONDITION [X] — DEFECTS LIABILITY PERIOD

The Defects Liability Period specified in [Annexure / Schedule] 
is amended to [24 / 36] months from the Date of Practical Completion.

[For building elements with extended warranty requirements:]
Notwithstanding the above, the Defects Liability Period for [waterproofing 
/ façade / roof] works shall be [5 / 7] years from the Date of 
Practical Completion.
```

### 3. Insurance Requirements

```
SPECIAL CONDITION [X] — INSURANCE

Clause [17 / 18] is amended to require the following minimum insurance:

(a) Contract Works Insurance: Not less than the full replacement value 
    of the Works plus [15]% for demolition and professional fees.

(b) Public Liability Insurance: Not less than $[20,000,000] per occurrence.

(c) Professional Indemnity Insurance (if D&C or design elements): 
    Not less than $[5,000,000] per claim and in aggregate.

(d) Workers Compensation Insurance: As required by law.

All policies must: name the Principal as an additional insured, 
not be cancellable without [30] days prior written notice to the Principal, 
and be maintained until [end of DLP / end of PI tail].
```

### 4. Key Personnel

```
SPECIAL CONDITION [X] — KEY PERSONNEL

(a) The following personnel are designated as Key Personnel:
    - Project Manager: [name]
    - Site Manager: [name]

(b) Key Personnel shall not be substituted without the prior written 
    consent of the Superintendent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

(c) Consent to substitution shall not be given unless the proposed 
    replacement has equivalent or greater qualifications and experience.

(d) The Principal may request removal of a Key Person for reasonable cause. 
    The Contractor shall replace within [14] days.
```

### 5. Programme Requirements

```
SPECIAL CONDITION [X] — PROGRAMME

Clause [33 / equivalent] is amended as follows:

(a) The Contractor shall submit a detailed baseline programme within 
    [10] business days of executing the Contract.

(b) The programme shall show: critical path, all activities with 
    durations and logic links, milestones, long-lead procurement, 
    resource allocation.

(c) The Contractor shall update and resubmit the programme [monthly / 
    fortnightly] and within [5] business days of any event affecting 
    the critical path.

(d) Failure to submit the programme or updates on time entitles the 
    Principal to withhold [X]% of each progress payment until complied with.
```

### 6. Retention Terms

```
SPECIAL CONDITION [X] — RETENTION

Clause [37.4 / 42.2] is amended as follows:

(a) Retention shall be deducted at [5]% of each progress payment 
    until the retention fund equals [5]% of the Contract Sum.

(b) On the Date of Practical Completion, [half / all] of the retention 
    fund shall be released to the Contractor.

(c) The balance of the retention fund shall be released at the end 
    of the Defects Liability Period, subject to all notified defects 
    being rectified.
```

### 7. Novation of Consultants (D&C)

```
SPECIAL CONDITION [X] — NOVATION OF CONSULTANTS

(a) The Principal shall novate the following consultants to the 
    Contractor at the Date of the Contract:
    - [Consultant name, discipline, agreement date]

(b) Novation shall be effected by a Deed of Novation in the form 
    attached as Annexure [X].

(c) The Contractor shall be bound by and assume all obligations of 
    the Principal under the novated consultancy agreements from the 
    Date of the Contract.

(d) The Principal warrants that there are no undisclosed defaults 
    under the consultancy agreements as at the Date of the Contract.
```

## Security of Payment — NSW Overview

The Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 1999 (NSW) (SOP Act) applies to all construction contracts in NSW. Key provisions that affect contract administration:

| Provision | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Payment claims | Contractor can serve a payment claim under the SOP Act at any time (not limited to contract payment schedule) |
| Response time | Principal must provide a payment schedule within 10 business days (or the shorter of 10 business days / period in contract) |
| Adjudication | If disputed, either party can seek adjudication — determination within 10 business days |
| Enforceability | Adjudication determinations are enforceable as court judgments |
| Cannot contract out | Provisions that purport to exclude SOP Act rights are void |

**Practical implication:** Even if the contract says "payment within 28 days", a payment claim served under the SOP Act requires a response within 10 business days. Failure to respond = deemed acceptance of the claimed amount. Alert the Delivery Agent to this at contract award.
