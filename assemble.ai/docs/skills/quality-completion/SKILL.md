# Quality and Completion Skill

## When to Load

Load this skill when:
- The contractor has submitted or is about to submit a PC claim
- Conducting a practical completion inspection
- Administering defects during the Defects Liability Period (DLP)
- Preparing the final completion certificate at end of DLP
- Coordinating occupation certificate or CDC final inspection

---

## Part 1: Practical Completion

### What Is Practical Completion?

Under Australian standard form contracts (AS 4000, AS 2124, AS 4902), Practical Completion (PC) occurs when:
- The works are **substantially complete** — fit for their intended purpose
- Minor defects may exist but do **not prevent reasonable use** of the works
- All required statutory approvals are in place (occupation certificate, fire safety certificate)
- All required commissioning, testing, and certification has been completed

PC is not perfection. The standard is "substantially complete" — minor items can be outstanding and recorded in a defects list. However, major defects or incomplete works prevent PC.

### Pre-PC Actions (2–4 weeks before anticipated PC)

Start preparing before the contractor submits their PC claim:

```
PRE-PC PREPARATION CHECKLIST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
☐ Confirm anticipated PC date with contractor and Program Agent
☐ Arrange PC inspection — date, attendees (Superintendent, contractor, 
  key consultants, certifier)
☐ Confirm certifier (PCA) has all required inspection hold points cleared
☐ Request from contractor: as-built drawings, O&M manuals, warranties, 
  guarantees — allow 2 weeks for review
☐ Confirm occupation certificate / CDC status with certifier
☐ Brief Finance Agent: PC triggers retention release, DLP insurance, 
  potential LD finalisation
☐ Brief Program Agent: actual PC date to be recorded against programme
```

### PC Checklist

Work through each item at the PC inspection. Items marked ✗ outstanding must be resolved before PC unless they are genuinely minor defects.

#### Statutory and Certification

```
☐ Occupation Certificate (OC) issued [or CDC final inspection cleared]
☐ Fire Safety Certificate issued and submitted to council
☐ Essential services commissioning certificates received:
    ☐ Fire sprinkler system
    ☐ Fire hydrant system
    ☐ Emergency warning and intercommunication system (EWIS)
    ☐ Emergency lighting and exit signs
    ☐ Mechanical ventilation / smoke exhaust
    ☐ Passenger lifts (SafeWork NSW certificate)
☐ BCA/NCC compliance confirmed by certifier
☐ All DA conditions discharged (check conditions register — no outstanding 
  "prior to occupation" conditions)
```

#### Structure and Fabric

```
☐ All structural works complete — no outstanding structural elements
☐ External envelope complete — no gaps, penetrations, or unfinished cladding
☐ Roof complete and waterproof (certifier's inspection passed)
☐ All windows and external doors installed, sealed, and operational
☐ Waterproofing complete and certified (wet areas, balconies, podium decks)
☐ All internal walls, ceilings, and floors complete (or minor finishing only)
```

#### Services

```
☐ Mechanical (HVAC) commissioned and balanced — commissioning report received
☐ Electrical services tested and tagged — test and tag reports received
☐ Hydraulic services tested and certified — plumbing compliance certificate
☐ Fire services commissioned — fire engineer sign-off received
☐ Lift commissioned and SafeWork certificate received
☐ Security / access control system operational and tested
☐ CCTV operational
☐ Communications / data cabling installed and tested
☐ Pool / spa (if applicable) — health certificate, safety barrier compliance
☐ Stormwater system connected, operational, and council-compliant
☐ Gas services tested and certified (if applicable)
```

#### Site and External Works

```
☐ Car park line marking, signage, and numbering complete
☐ Landscaping complete (or bond in lieu if seasonal planting deferred)
☐ All temporary works removed (hoardings, scaffolding, site sheds)
☐ Site clean — all construction waste removed
☐ Fencing and security operational
☐ Letterboxes installed and numbered (residential)
☐ Council footpath and road reinstatement complete
```

#### Documentation Package

```
☐ As-built drawings received and reviewed (all disciplines)
☐ Operation and Maintenance (O&M) manuals received (all services)
☐ Warranties received:
    ☐ Structural warranty (if applicable)
    ☐ Waterproofing warranty (minimum 10 years)
    ☐ Roof warranty
    ☐ Façade / cladding warranty
    ☐ Mechanical equipment warranties
    ☐ Lift warranty
    ☐ Any other manufacturer warranties specified in the contract
☐ Guarantees received (as required by contract)
☐ Subcontractor completion certificates received (if required)
☐ Builder's all-risk insurance confirmed to be maintained through DLP
☐ Keys, swipe cards, access codes, remote controls handed over
☐ Spare tiles, paint, materials (as specified) handed over
```

#### Contract-Specific Items

```
☐ Provisional sum expenditure reconciled
☐ Variation log reconciled — all approved variations accounted for
☐ RFI register closed — all RFIs responded to
☐ Site instruction register reconciled
☐ All contractor-issued notices responded to
```

(Integrated with Delivery Agent PC format — this skill provides the checklist inputs)

After completing the checklist, prepare the PC Assessment using the Delivery Agent's PC Assessment Format, incorporating:

## Part 2: Defects Liability Period (DLP)

### DLP Administration

The DLP begins on the date of Practical Completion. Duration is set in the contract Annexure — typically 12 months for commercial/residential; 24 months for major infrastructure; longer for specialist elements (waterproofing, structure).

### DLP Tracking

Set up a DLP tracking record immediately upon issuing the PC Certificate:

```
DLP TRACKING RECORD
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Project: [Name]
Date of Practical Completion: [date]
DLP Duration: [12 / 24] months
DLP Expiry Date: [date]
Contract clause: [AS 4000 Clause 35 / AS 2124 Clause 37]

INSPECTIONS SCHEDULED:
☐ 3-month DLP inspection: [date]
☐ 6-month DLP inspection: [date]  
☐ 11-month DLP inspection: [date] ← most important — final chance to identify latent defects
☐ DLP expiry inspection: [date]

FINANCE AGENT NOTIFICATIONS:
☐ Retention (second half) scheduled for release at: [DLP expiry date]
☐ Security (second half) scheduled for release at: [DLP expiry date]
```

### Defect Notification

When a defect is identified during the DLP:
1. Record in the defects register
2. Issue written notice to the Contractor via Correspondence Agent (with photos if available)
3. Set a rectification timeframe (typically 14–28 days for minor defects; urgent defects: 24–48 hours)
4. Follow up if not rectified within the stated period

**Defect Notification Letter (via Correspondence Agent):**
```
Subject: Defect Notice — [Project Name] — [Defect No]

We refer to the above project and notify you of the following defect 
identified during the Defects Liability Period:

Defect No: [number]
Location: [location]
Description: [description]
Date identified: [date]

Under Clause [35 / 37] of the Contract, you are required to rectify 
this defect within [14 / 28] days of this notice.

Please confirm receipt and your proposed rectification timeline.
```

### DLP Inspections

Conduct formal DLP inspections at 3, 6, and 11 months. The **11-month inspection** is critical — it is the last opportunity to identify and formally notify defects before the DLP expires.

**Inspection Focus Areas (by category):**

| Category | What to Check |
|----------|--------------|
| Waterproofing | Wet areas, balconies, roof — any leaks, bubbling, delamination |
| Façade | Cracking, sealant failure, corrosion, water ingress |
| Structural | Settlement cracks, beam deflection, slab cracking |
| Services | HVAC performance, hydraulic issues, electrical faults |
| Finishes | Paint failure, tile cracking, joinery issues, door operation |
| External | Pavement settlement, landscaping establishment, drainage performance |

---

### Failure to Rectify

If the Contractor fails to rectify a notified defect within the required timeframe:

1. Issue a follow-up notice (via Correspondence Agent)
2. If still not rectified: issue notice of intention to engage alternative contractor
3. Have the defect rectified by an alternative contractor
4. Recover the cost from the Contractor (from retention, security, or as a debt)

**AS 4000 Clause 35.4:** If Contractor fails to rectify within a reasonable time, Superintendent may arrange rectification and deduct cost from money due to Contractor.

## Part 3: Final Completion

### What Is Final Completion?

Final Completion occurs at the end of the DLP when:
- All defects notified during the DLP have been rectified
- All final documentation has been received
- The final certificate is issued by the Superintendent

### Final Completion Checklist

```
FINAL COMPLETION CHECKLIST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Project: [Name]
DLP Expiry: [date]
Final Inspection Date: [date]

☐ All defects notified during DLP have been rectified and closed in register
☐ Final DLP inspection completed — no outstanding defects
☐ Any updated as-built drawings received (for changes made during DLP)
☐ Updated warranty documents received (any warranty claims resolved)
☐ No outstanding correspondence requiring response
☐ Contractor has no outstanding claims (or all claims resolved)
☐ Finance Agent confirms: final account ready for preparation
```

### Final Certificate

Upon Final Completion, Superintendent issues the Final Certificate:

```
FINAL CERTIFICATE — DRAFT FOR REVIEW
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Project: [Name]
Contract: [form and date]
Contractor: [name]

This certifies that:
1. The Defects Liability Period expired on [date].
2. All defects notified during the DLP have been satisfactorily rectified.
3. The works are complete in accordance with the Contract.

Final amounts certified:
- Original Contract Sum: $[amount]
- Approved Variations: $[amount]
- Adjusted Contract Sum: $[amount]
- Previously certified: $[amount]
- Retention released (this certificate): $[amount]
- AMOUNT OF THIS FINAL CERTIFICATE: $[amount]

⚠ DRAFT FOR YOUR REVIEW

→ On issuing this certificate:
  Finance Agent will be notified to release remaining retention and 
  prepare the Final Account.
  Program Agent will record actual Final Completion date.
```

### Triggering the Final Account

After issuing the Final Certificate, trigger the Finance Agent:

```
Finance Agent, the Final Certificate has been issued for [Project Name].
Please prepare the Final Account reconciling:
- Original contract sum: $[amount]
- All approved variations: $[amount] ([X] variations)
- Provisional sum adjustments: $[amount]
- Progress certificates issued: $[amount]
- Retention released: $[amount]
- Final payment due: $[amount]
```

## Part 4: Occupation Certificate Coordination

### Types of OC (NSW)

| Type | When | Issued By |
|------|------|-----------|
| Interim OC | Part of the building complete (e.g., lower floors) | PCA (certifier) |
| Final OC | Entire building complete | PCA (certifier) |
| CDC Final Inspection | For Complying Development | Private certifier |

### OC Coordination Checklist

```
☐ Certifier has completed all mandatory inspections (check inspection schedule)
☐ Fire safety certificate submitted to council / certifier
☐ Essential services commissioning certificates provided to certifier
☐ BCA compliance documentation complete
☐ Any outstanding DA conditions addressed (confirm with Design Agent)
☐ Stormwater connection certified
☐ Structural inspections complete
☐ Accessibility compliance confirmed
☐ BASIX commitments verified as implemented (residential)
```

**Note:** PC cannot be certified if the OC has not been issued (or is not imminent). If the certifier has an unresolved issue preventing OC, PC cannot be achieved. Escalate to Design Agent and notify Program Agent of the delay.

## Tone Notes for This Skill

- Present the PC checklist systematically — do not rush or abbreviate
- Flag any item where the status is unclear: "Status unknown — confirm with contractor / certifier"
- At the 11-month DLP inspection, be thorough — this is the last formal opportunity before the Contractor's obligations expire
- When triggering Final Account with Finance Agent, be precise about the numbers — final account reconciliation is a formal contractual step
