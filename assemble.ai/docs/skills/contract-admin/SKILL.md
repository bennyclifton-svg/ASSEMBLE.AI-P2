# Contract Administration Skill

## Tier 3 — Contract Clause Reference Material Loaded

This skill provides detailed clause reference tables for the four major Australian standard form contracts. Load this skill when administering a contract under any of these forms. The Delivery Agent uses this to reference specific clauses in assessments, notices, and recommendations.

**Always advise the user:**
> "Clause references are based on the standard edition noted. Verify against the specific edition and any special conditions used on the project — special conditions may amend, delete, or replace standard clauses."

**If a contractual matter is complex, high-value, or novel:**
> "This matter warrants legal review before making a determination."

---

## How to Use This Skill

1. Identify the applicable contract form from `settings.json` → `contract_form`
2. Navigate to the relevant section below
3. Reference the clause in your assessment, notice, or recommendation
4. Always cross-check against the executed contract's Annexure / Schedule — numerical values (LD rates, retention %, DLP duration) are project-specific

## AS 4000-1997 — Complete Administration Reference

### Superintendent

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 20.1 | Role | Superintendent acts as Principal's agent except where required to act as independent certifier (payment, EOT, PC). |
| 20.2 | Independence | When certifying, Superintendent must act honestly, fairly, and not as advocate for either party. |
| 20.3 | Delegation | Superintendent may delegate. Contractor must be notified. |
| 20.4 | Communications | All directions in writing. Oral directions confirmed in writing within 2 business days. |

### Security

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 5.1 | Security amount | Set in Annexure Part A — typically 5% of contract sum. |
| 5.2 | Form | Bank guarantee or cash retention. |
| 5.3 | Return | Half at PC; remainder at end of DLP. |
| 5.4 | Recourse | Principal may have recourse to security if Contractor defaults. |

### Insurance

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 17.1 | Works insurance | Contractor insures works to agreed value. Joint names policy. |
| 17.2 | Public liability | Contractor — minimum amount in Annexure (typically $20M+). |
| 17.3 | Workers compensation | Contractor and subcontractors — as required by law. |
| 18 | Principal's insurance | Principal maintains professional indemnity if Principal-furnished design. |
| 17.6 | Evidence | Contractor must provide certificates before commencing. |

### Subcontracting

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 9.1 | Right to subcontract | Contractor may subcontract but remains responsible for all works. |
| 9.2 | Nominated subcontractors | If nominated, Contractor must engage unless reasonable objection. |
| 9.3 | Objection to nominated sub | Contractor notifies within 5 business days. |

### Programme

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 33.1 | Programme submission | Within time stated in Annexure (typically 10–14 days of award). |
| 33.2 | Programme content | Activities, logic links, critical path, milestones, resource loading. |
| 33.3 | Programme updates | On request or when revised. Typically monthly in special conditions. |
| 33.4 | Programme not approval | Superintendent's receipt of programme is not approval of methods. |

### Variations

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 36.1 | Direction to vary | Superintendent may direct variations in writing. Must be within general scope. |
| 36.2 | Contractor's obligation | Contractor must comply with variation direction even if disputed. |
| 36.3 | Valuation — agreement | By agreement between parties first. |
| 36.3(a) | Valuation — contract rates | Where applicable rates exist in contract. |
| 36.3(b) | Valuation — reasonable rates | Where no applicable contract rates. |
| 36.3(c) | Valuation — daywork | Where other methods inappropriate. Rates in Annexure. |
| 36.4 | Contractor-proposed variation | Contractor may propose; Superintendent may accept or reject. |
| 36.5 | Disputed valuation | Contractor proceeds; dispute resolved through dispute resolution. |
| 36.6 | Provisional sums | Superintendent directs expenditure. Actual cost + margin replaces PS. |
| 36.7 | Rise and fall | If rise and fall applies — as stated in Annexure. |

**Variation Entitlement Checklist (AS 4000):**
- Was it directed by the Superintendent in writing? → Entitlement established (Clause 36.1)
- If not directed: is it a latent condition? → Clause 25
- Is it within the general scope of the contract? → If outside scope, no entitlement
- Was notice given if required? → Check special conditions for notice requirements
- Is it already included in the contract scope? → If yes, no entitlement

### Extensions of Time

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 34.1 | Qualifying causes | (a) Principal's act or omission; (b) latent conditions; (c) industrial action; (d) exceptionally adverse weather; (e) other causes stated in Annexure. |
| 34.2 | Notice requirement | Contractor gives notice within 28 days of the cause of delay becoming known. |
| 34.3 | Content of notice | Description of cause, likely delay, steps to mitigate. |
| 34.4 | Superintendent's assessment | Must grant EOT if qualifying cause established and delay occurred. May not reduce time (no negative EOT). |
| 34.5 | Assessment timing | Superintendent may assess at any time; final assessment at PC. |
| 34.6 | Practical completion | Date for PC adjusted by granted EOTs. |
| 34.7 | Liquidated damages | Per diem/week rate in Annexure. Accrues from PC date until actual PC. |
| 34.8 | LD as genuine estimate | LD must be a genuine pre-estimate of loss, not a penalty. |

**EOT Qualifying Causes Quick Reference (AS 4000 Clause 34.1):**
1. Principal's act or omission (including late instructions, changed access, Principal-caused delay)
2. Latent conditions (Clause 25 claim established)
3. Industrial action not caused by Contractor's default
4. Exceptionally adverse weather (beyond what could reasonably be anticipated)
5. Any other cause stated in Annexure Part A

**EOT Notice — 28-Day Rule:**
Notice must be given within 28 days of the cause becoming apparent. Late notice does not necessarily extinguish the claim but weakens it. Assess whether the Principal was prejudiced by late notice.

### Latent Conditions

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 25.1 | Definition | Physical conditions on site differing materially from those that a contractor could reasonably have anticipated from the contract documents, inspections, and general knowledge. |
| 25.2 | Notice | Contractor gives notice as soon as practicable after discovery. |
| 25.3 | Mitigation | Contractor takes steps to minimise delay and cost impact. |
| 25.4 | Entitlement | If latent condition established: time (EOT via Clause 34) and cost (variation via Clause 36). |
| 25.5 | Assessment | Superintendent assesses whether conditions were reasonably anticipatable. |

**Latent Condition Assessment Steps:**
1. What did the contract documents reveal about site conditions?
2. What was available from a reasonable site inspection?
3. What would a competent contractor in the market have known/anticipated?
4. Do the actual conditions materially differ from what was reasonably anticipatable?
5. Did the Contractor give notice as soon as practicable?

### Progress Claims and Payment

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 37.1 | Payment claim | Contractor submits monthly (or per Annexure schedule). |
| 37.2 | Superintendent's certificate | Within 14 days of receiving claim. Must certify amount properly due. |
| 37.3 | Payment | Principal pays within 28 days of claim (SOP Act governs if shorter). |
| 37.4 | Retention | Per Annexure — typically 5% to limit of 5% contract sum. |
| 37.5 | Unfixed materials | Claimable if delivered to site and protected, per Annexure. |
| 37.6 | Disputed amounts | Superintendent certifies undisputed amount; dispute resolved through dispute resolution. |
| 37.7 | Interest | On late payments — rate in Annexure. |

**SOP Act Override (NSW):**
Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 1999 (NSW). Payment schedule must be provided within 10 business days of receiving payment claim. Principal must pay by the due date stated in the payment schedule. Failure to provide payment schedule = full claimed amount becomes debt.

### Practical Completion

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 34.6 | When PC occurs | Works are complete except for minor defects that do not prevent reasonable use. |
| 34.6 | Certificate | Superintendent issues PC Certificate stating date of PC. |
| 34.6 | Defects at PC | Minor defects noted in defects list — do not prevent PC if do not affect use. |
| 35.1 | DLP commencement | Starts from date of PC. Duration in Annexure (typically 12 months). |
| 35.2 | Defect notification | Superintendent notifies defects during DLP. |
| 35.3 | Contractor's obligation | Rectify notified defects during DLP at contractor's cost. |
| 35.4 | Failure to rectify | Principal may have defects rectified and recover cost from Contractor. |
| 35.5 | Final certificate | Issued after DLP and rectification of all defects. |

### Suspension

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 34.5 | Suspension by Principal | For convenience. Contractor entitled to costs and reasonable profit on suspended work, plus EOT. |
| 34.5 | Suspension by Contractor | If Principal fails to pay — after 14 days written notice. |

### Termination

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 39.1 | Default by Contractor | Principal may give 7 days notice to remedy. If not remedied, terminate. |
| 39.2 | Show cause | Before termination for default, Principal may (and often should) issue show cause notice. |
| 39.3 | Termination for convenience | Principal may terminate on 14 days notice. Contractor entitled to: work done + loss of profit on uncompleted work. |
| 39.4 | Contractor's rights on termination | Remove plant and materials. Dispute determination through dispute resolution. |
| 40 | Insolvency | Automatic termination provisions if Contractor becomes insolvent. |

### Dispute Resolution

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 42.1 | Notice of dispute | Party notifies dispute in writing. |
| 42.2 | Expert determination | Independent expert appointed. Determination binding unless appealed to arbitration within 28 days. |
| 42.3 | Arbitration | If expert determination appealed. Binding. |
| 42.4 | Continue working | Contractor must continue working during dispute. |

## AS 2124-1992 — Complete Administration Reference

### Key Differences from AS 4000

| Topic | AS 2124 | AS 4000 |
|-------|---------|---------|
| EOT notice | "Within a reasonable time" | "28 days" (more certain) |
| Retention | Up to 10% → 5% at PC | Up to 5% → 2.5% at PC (typically) |
| Nominated subcontractors | More detailed provisions (Clause 9A) | Simpler |
| Superintendent's independence | Clause 23 — similar to AS 4000 Clause 20 | Clause 20 |

### AS 2124 Key Clauses

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 7 | Security | Bank guarantee or retention. Amount and form in Annexure. |
| 12 | Latent conditions | Physical conditions materially different from reasonably anticipatable. Notice "as soon as practicable". |
| 23 | Superintendent | Acts as Principal's agent except when required to act independently. |
| 23.1 | Directions | In writing. Oral confirmed in writing within 2 days. |
| 33 | Programme | Submit within time in Annexure. Update on request. |
| 35 | EOT | Qualifying causes: Principal default, latent conditions, industrial action, weather, other causes in Annexure. Notice "within a reasonable time". |
| 36 | Liquidated damages | Rate in Annexure. Deducted from payment certificates. |
| 37 | Defects liability | DLP from PC. Duration in Annexure. Notify and rectify. |
| 40 | Variations | Direction in writing. Valuation by agreement, contract rates, reasonable rates, or daywork. |
| 40.5 | Variation valuation | Agreement first; contract rates; reasonable rates; daywork. |
| 42 | Payment | Monthly claims. Certify within 14 days. Pay within 28 days (SOP Act governs). |
| 42.1 | Retention | Typically 10% reducing to 5% at PC. Half released at PC, remainder at DLP end. |
| 42.5 | Practical completion | Superintendent issues certificate. Minor defects permitted. |
| 44 | Termination | Default (7 days notice) or convenience (14 days). |
| 47 | Dispute resolution | Expert determination → arbitration. |

## AS 4902-2000 — Complete Administration Reference

### D&C-Specific Clauses

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 6 | Novation of consultants | Principal's consultants novated to Contractor at award. Contractor then administers. |
| 8.1 | Design obligation | Contractor responsible for all design. Must be fit for stated purpose — higher than reasonable care. |
| 8.2 | Design documents | Contractor submits design documents for review (not approval). Superintendent may comment within 10 business days. |
| 8.3 | Intellectual property | Contractor retains IP; grants Principal broad licence to use. |
| 8.4 | Changes to client requirements | Only through variation direction. Principal cannot require design changes outside variation mechanism. |
| 20 | Superintendent | Same as AS 4000 but reduced design-direction powers. |
| 25 | Latent conditions | Same as AS 4000. |
| 34 | EOT | Same qualifying causes. Design delays caused by Contractor's own consultants are Contractor's risk. |
| 35 | DLP | Extended latent defect exposure — design defects may manifest beyond standard DLP. |
| 36 | Variations | Scope changes only. Superintendent cannot direct design variations — only changes to Client's Project Requirements. |
| 37 | Payment | Same as AS 4000. |

**Key D&C Administration Point:**
The Contractor bears design risk. If the design fails to meet the fitness for purpose standard, it is a Contractor defect regardless of whether the Superintendent reviewed the design documents. "Review" under AS 4902 Clause 8.2 is not approval.

## ABIC MW-1 — Complete Administration Reference

### ABIC Key Differences

ABIC MW-1 is architect-administered. The Architect replaces the Superintendent as contract administrator. Powers are more limited than AS 4000.

| Clause | Topic | Key Content |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| A5 | Architect's role | Administrator, not agent of Principal. Acts independently when certifying. |
| A5.2 | Directions | In writing. |
| B1 | Contract documents | Listed in the contract. |
| C3 | EOT | Qualifying causes: Principal default, weather, industrial action. Notice "promptly". |
| C4 | Practical completion | Architect issues PC certificate. Minor defects noted. |
| C5 | Defects | DLP from PC. Notify and rectify. Typically 12 months. |
| D4 | Variations | Architect issues variation orders. Contractor complies. |
| D4.3 | Valuation | Agreement; contract rates; reasonable rates. |
| E2 | Progress claims | Monthly. Architect certifies within 10 business days. Principal pays within 15 business days of certificate. |
| E3 | Retention | Per schedule. Typically 5% to limit. Half at PC, remainder at DLP end. |
| J | Termination | Default (7 days notice) or convenience. |
| K | Dispute resolution | Expert determination. |

## Common Administration Traps

| Trap | Risk | Prevention |
|------|------|-----------|
| Missing EOT notice window | Contractor loses entitlement (or entitlement is weakened) | Log cause of delay date and notice deadline when delay events occur |
| Certifying late (AS 4000 Cl 37.2) | SOP Act: full claimed amount becomes payable | Set calendar reminders for certification deadlines on every claim |
| Not issuing payment schedule (SOP Act) | Full claimed amount is undisputed debt — no ability to withhold | Never let a payment claim sit unresponded for 10 business days |
| Verbal variation directions | Not binding. Contractor may claim without written direction. | Confirm all verbal discussions in writing within 2 days (AS 4000 Cl 20.4) |
| Accepting contractor's nominee without checking | Key personnel substituted without consent | Flag key personnel clause at contract award; monitor site staffing |
| Not reading the Annexure | LD rate, retention %, DLP duration — all in Annexure, not the body | Read the executed Annexure first before every major assessment |
| Certifying PC with major defects outstanding | Creates legal dispute about whether PC was properly achieved | Apply PC checklist; only minor defects permitted at PC |
| Show cause before termination | Terminating without show cause may be unlawful termination | Always issue show cause notice and wait for response before terminating |
